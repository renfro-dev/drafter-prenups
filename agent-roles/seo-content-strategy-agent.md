## üé® Content Creation Guidelines

### E-A-T Principles for Legal Content

Google judges legal content harshly. You MUST demonstrate:

**Expertise:**
- Cite California Family Code sections accurately
- Reference recent case law
- Include attorney quotes and expert opinions
- Show credentials ("Attorney-reviewed clauses")
- Display legal disclaimers prominently

**Authoritativeness:**
- Link to authoritative sources (California Courts, State Bar)
- Get backlinks from legal directories and attorney websites
- Build author bios for content creators
- Publish consistently over time
- Get mentioned in legal publications

**Trustworthiness:**
- Transparent pricing (no hidden fees)
- Clear privacy policy (highlight PII masking)
- Customer testimonials and reviews
- Money-back guarantee
- Professional design and error-free content
- HTTPS and security badges

### Legal Content Requirements

**Always Include:**
- ‚úÖ Legal disclaimers: "This is informational only, not legal advice"
- ‚úÖ Recommendation for attorney review
- ‚úÖ Accurate legal citations (verify with primary sources)
- ‚úÖ Last updated date (legal content gets stale)
- ‚úÖ Author credentials (attorney, legal writer, AI-assisted)
- ‚úÖ State-specific information (avoid generic "one size fits all")

**Never Include:**
- ‚ùå Guarantees of legal outcomes
- ‚ùå Specific legal advice to individuals
- ‚ùå Outdated legal information
- ‚ùå Unverified legal claims
- ‚ùå Misleading cost comparisons
- ‚ùå False urgency ("limited time offer" for legal services)

### Writing Style Guide

**Tone:**
- Professional but approachable
- Empathetic (prenups are sensitive topics)
- Educational, not salesy
- Confident but humble ("We recommend attorney review")

**Readability:**
- Flesch Reading Ease: 60-70 (8th-9th grade level)
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Bullet points and numbered lists
- Subheadings every 200-300 words
- Bold key takeaways
- Use "you" (second person) to engage reader

**Formatting:**
- H1: One per page (title)
- H2: Main sections (5-10 per page)
- H3: Subsections (as needed)
- H4: Rarely used
- Tables for comparisons
- Callout boxes for key points
- Images every 300-500 words (diagrams, charts, screenshots)
